Our Vision & Commitment

Our Vision is to encourage the development of football at all levels, with the emphasis of giving priority to the communities on the Cape Flats.

Our commitment is to use football as a vehicle to build young peopleâ€™s self-confidence, self-worth and self-esteem. Develop team work and leadership skills. Help young people discover purpose and set life goals. Encourage respect for the opposition and others in general, include players of all abilities and skill sets but above all to have fun.

Our Mission

The mission of First National Rangers St. Agnes Amateur Football Club is to encourage and promote the development of football on the Cape Flats, Cape Town, with the emphasis on youth football and to provide our young players an avenue away from the scourge of gangsterism and drugs in their communities.

The Club will strive to provide an opportunity to all players regardless of their background, ethnicity, religion, gender and social standing. The Club will seek to provide the best possible playing facilities, training and playing equipment available to us to grow the sport of football on the Cape Flat

features

Juniors

"TEAM WORK MAKES THE DREAM WORK - UNKNOWN" .

The Club has five junior teams, which consist of a under 10, 11, 12, 13A and 13B teams.

Most of our junior members come from the sub-economic areas on the Cape Flats. in Cape Town. Which is well known for its gangsterism and drugs.

Besides teaching and coaching the game of soccer, our staff must sometimes act as the social worker, parent and guidance teacher. We do this in order to create the right environment for them to thrive in and to show them another way away from their normal everyday experiences.

For the majority we provide transport to and from training and/or matches on match days. In many cases, we provide the children with food in order for them to be nourished and sustained whilst they are in the Club's care.

Seniors

"IF YOU WANT TO BE THE BEST, YOU HAVE TO DO THE THINGS OTHER PEOPLE AREN'T WILLING TO DO" - MICHAEL PHELPS..

First National Rangers St. Agnes Football Club has three senior teams, which consists of the SAFA SAB (Summer) League, the Cape Town Tygerberg Football Association (CTTFA) Premier and Premier Reserve Teams.

There teams play in the winter league. The Premier Reserve team is primarily the team who fast tracks youth team players to Premier and SAB teams.

Quite a number of youth players are now regular players in the above mentioned teams.

Veterans

"THERE'S NO TRAFFIC ON THE EXTRA MILE" - UNKNOWN

First National Rangers St Agnes FC has four Veteran teams which consist of over 35A, Over40A, Over 45A and Over 45B Teams.

These teams are for the most part players who have been with the Club for many, many years, who have played in the Club's top teams as well as having played professional football in South Africa.

The veterans are extremely competitive and almost every year are among the trophy winners in the CTTFA Leagues. They are also the life blood of the club in terms of having more disposable cash than youth and senior players.

Many of them are involved in the Junior and Youth structures of the club where they impart invaluable knowledge to the young players in respect of discipline, playing techniques and life skills.

Youths

"TOUGH TIMES DON'T LAST, TOUGH TEAMS DO" - UNKNOWN

Our youth teams range from under 14 to under 18 year old players. Most of our youth players have been with the club since juniors and have been progressed through the ranks. Some are now regular players in the SAB League, Premier & Premier Reserve Teams.

The majority of them come from the townships on the Cape Flats, in Cape Town. It is very important to keep these youth players busy with football because they are at the stage of their lives where they are very impressionable and vulnerable to the lifestyle of gangsterism and easy money as well as drug abuse.

To this end do we fetch them for soccer practice and transport them back home. We do the same on match days. Some of them have moved onto professional clubs. Our club also assists some players from time to time with school educational and nutritional needs.

Our History

1947 - Rangers FC was formed by M.O.T.H and had its home ground at Malta Park, Observator. The clubhouse was demolished by the developer of the Adidas Complex at Fir Street, Observatory with permission from the City of Cape Town. Promises was made to build the clubhouse at another location at Malta Park but has up to date not materialised.

1948 - St Aagnes FC Formed in Woodstock and had its home at Malta Park.

1977 - The First National Football Club was Formed and played in the Cape Western Leagues Division.

1993 - The First National Rangers ST Agnes FC was formed and currently plays in the Cape Town Tygerberg LFA under the auspices of SAFA Cape Town.
